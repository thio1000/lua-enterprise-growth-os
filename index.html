06 · Personas & Buying Committees

Business Problem Library

The named job that's bleeding hours, by industry — not "an AI agent for X."

  • Banking: balance/loan-status enquiries, credit-application recommendation, KYC/AML document review, next-best-action from CRM
  • SACCO: loan balance and eligibility checks, document collection and routing, repayment collections and negotiation
  • Retail: inventory/pricing/supplier updates, order status, refunds, loyalty enrollment
  • Insurance: claims document intake and completeness screening, policy/premium explanation and renewals
  • Hospital: appointment scheduling, billing and insurance guidance, claims status
  • Telecom: bundle/data queries — with a mandatory, always-reachable human escalation path. The Zuri lawsuit is the direct lesson: the product requirement isn't "automate the query," it's "automate the query and never trap the customer in the flow"
  • Education: admissions-status queries, cluster-point/placement queries, portal-downtime overflow handling
  • Government/Utility: outage and billing queries, tax cross-validation status — real pain, but sequenced last due to procurement cycle length
  • Agriculture / NGO: genuinely under-explored. no data found — the honest move is a discovery-led conversation, not an assumed use case

12 · Metrics & Operating System

North Star Metrics

Evaluated against Kenya's real sales-cycle reality, not against a generic SaaS metrics list.

Candidates considered

CandidateVerdict
Enterprise AI Adoption RateToo vague and lagging — hard to attribute to commercial effort specifically
Time to Paid PilotStrong leading indicator, matches the 90-day KPI for this role — but doesn't capture whether the pilot actually delivered value
Pilot-to-Production ConversionCritical, but this is a company-wide product+delivery metric more than a purely commercial one
Expansion VelocityThe right metric once a base of production clients exists — too early for a market-opening role in year one
Business Processes AutomatedGood qualitative proxy (count of live Digital Employees) but not inherently commercial
Weekly Active Enterprise AgentsA product-usage metric, not the right North Star for the commercial function
Executive Adoption ScoreSoft, hard to define reliably — real risk of becoming a vanity metric
Recommended North Star: Time to First Business Value

Not "signed" — the first measurable outcome the Digital Employee actually delivers (first N enquiries handled with a clean human-escalation record, first claim routed, first loan processed). This is the truest proxy for "adoption over awareness": it forces the retail/SACCO-first sequencing, and it directly answers the Zuri lesson — a fast signature with a broken escalation path is not a win.

Supporting metric tree

  • Leading: Time to Paid Pilot (near-term, matches the JD's 90-day KPI)
  • North Star: Time to First Business Value
  • Lagging: Pilot-to-Production Conversion (mid-term), Expansion Velocity (year two, once a client base exists)

The 90-day KPI, scoped honestly against everything above.

Map the real landscape

Confirm existing Kenya pipeline via the Head of Sub-Saharan Africa hire. Verify Lua's ODPC registration status before it's in front of a bank. Open 6–8 conversations across retail, SACCOs, and one or two insurers — deliberately not banks first.

Success6+ qualified conversations, 2+ scoped problem statements
DAY 31–60

Turn conversations into paid proposals

Convert 2–3 into scoped, paid audit or pilot proposals with the Implementation Lead.

Success1–2 signed paid audits/pilots in motion
DAY 61–90

Land the first signed, paid client

A retail or SACCO Digital Employee — not a bank contract. Start structured relationship-building with 1–2 banks/insurers in parallel.

Success1 signed + paid client live, matching the role's own 90-day measure
DAY 91–120

Expand and land a second logo

Second Digital Employee in the first account (Expansion Loop, Section 16); land a second logo; start the quarterly product-feedback loop (Section 10).

Success2 paying clients, 1 expansion, a repeatable discovery script
DAY 365

A market, not a lucky deal

3–5 signed clients across two near-term verticals. At least one bank or insurer moved from relationship to signed pilot. A documented East Africa playbook ready for the next regional hire.
